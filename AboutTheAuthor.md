# About the Author
Ben Tubbing was born in Rotterdam, the Netherlands, in 1957.

Married in 1988, he and his wife have three daughters.

**Studies**
- Applied Physics at Delft University
- PhD at the University of Utrecht on microwave diagnostics for measuring electron temperature in nuclear fusion devices
- Detached to the Joint European Torus (JET) in Culham, UK

**Professional (from 1987)**
- JET Physics Operations: running experiments on the machine
- Coordinating JET’s contributions to the design of ITER (International Thermonuclear Experimental Reactor)
- In 1998 moved to the European Commission, Brussels, Directorate‑General Research
- Research funding in advanced materials and nanotechnology
- Contributed to establishing the European Research Council

**Retirement (from 2022)**
- Continuing lifelong hobbies in mechanical engineering, electronics, software, furniture
- Designed and built a globally unique parametric‑excitation “free” pendulum
- Publications in the Horological Journal (2024, 2025, 2026)

  **Workshop**
- Myford Super 7B, upgraded EMCO F1-CNC, BambuLab X1 Carbon, Festool and Metabo woodworking saws
- Current work: a small portable clock with a Lavet-inspired motor and four clock-faces - hence this work on milling gears
